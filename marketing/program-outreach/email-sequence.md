# Program-Director Outreach — 3-Touch Email Sequence

Send individually (no BCC blasts). Personalize the **[bracketed]** line in each. Send from your real
name + a macprep.org address. Plain text, conversational — like emailing a colleague, not a brochure.

**Three points to land — but woven into normal sentences, never as a feature dump:**
1. Research-driven — a real research source on every question.
2. A full rationale on every answer choice — right *and* wrong.
3. One-time $50 for life — not a subscription — a fraction of the other options.

**Tone rule:** lead with the *why* (you built the thing you wished you'd had), keep it humble, and
actively invite criticism — asking for critique is what makes it not feel like a sales pitch.

## Notes
- **Timing:** best windows are the months before a cohort's CERT exam and the start of a new academic
  year. Avoid winter holidays.
- **If they reply interested:** just go set up the call/login — don't re-pitch.
- **If they pass:** ask to check back next cycle. A "no now" is often a "yes later."
- **Multi-channel:** for directors with no public email (see `program-directors-contacts.csv`), LinkedIn
  is the primary route — same warm tone, same three points woven in.
- **Never knock competitors** — let the research-sourcing, the per-choice rationale, and the $50-for-life
  price speak for themselves.
