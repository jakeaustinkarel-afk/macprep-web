# Program-Director Reply Playbook

Short, ready-to-send responses for the replies you'll actually get. Keep the warm, no-pressure tone.
Personalize `[First name]`. The goal of every reply: make the next step effortless for them.

---

## 1. "Sounds interesting — tell me more"
**→ Don't re-pitch. Get them into the product.**

> So glad it caught your eye, [First name]! Easiest thing is for me to just give you free access so you
> can click around yourself — want me to set that up? Takes me two minutes. And if it's helpful, I'm
> happy to hop on a quick 15 minutes to walk you (and any faculty) through it and answer anything.
>
> Quick gist in the meantime: every question is tied to a real research source you can click straight
> through to, every answer choice — right and wrong — has a full rationale, and for students it's a
> one-time $50 for life, no subscription.
>
> What's the best email to send your login to?

---

## 2. "How much is it / what's the catch?"
**→ Total transparency. There genuinely isn't a catch.**

> No catch, honestly. For you as a program, it's completely free — I'll set you up so you can use and
> evaluate it however helps. For students, it's a one-time $50 that lasts for life — not a subscription,
> no renewals, no upsells.
>
> I built it that way on purpose; I remember how brutal prep costs were when I came up, and I wanted
> something rigorous that students could actually afford. Happy to send you a login so you can see
> exactly what they'd get.

---

## 3. "We already use [Vigilant / another resource]"
**→ Be gracious. Never knock the competitor. Differentiate on substance, offer to complement.**

> Totally makes sense — there are some solid options out there, and I'd never tell you to drop something
> that's working for your students.
>
> The reason I built MACPrep was to do a couple of things differently: every question links to a real
> research source students can click straight through to (so they can verify the reasoning, not just
> take it on faith), and there's a full rationale on every answer choice — right and wrong. It's also a
> one-time $50 for life rather than a subscription.
>
> Even just as a complementary resource, I'd love to give you free access to see if it adds anything for
> your students — and honestly, I'd value your take comparing it to what you use now.

---

## 4. "Not right now"
**→ Keep the door open, leave on a warm note, set up a future touch.**

> Completely understand, [First name] — I know how much is on your plate, no worries at all.
>
> Mind if I check back next cycle in case the timing's better? And the offer stands anytime: if you ever
> want free access to take a look — or want me to send your students a free printable practice exam —
> just say the word.
>
> Thanks for taking the time to reply, and for everything you do for the field. It means a lot.

---

## Bonus: "Yes, send the login" / they're ready
**→ Move immediately. Speed wins.**

> Awesome — just sent your access to [email]. Poke around as much as you like; the full bank is open to
> you. If anything feels off or missing, tell me straight, I'm always improving it. And whenever you're
> ready, I'm glad to share a short blurb you can forward to your students so it's no work on your end.
> Thanks, [First name]!
