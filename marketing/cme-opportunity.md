# MACPrep CME — Opportunity Scoping

_Researched 2026-06-21. Verify regulatory specifics with NCCAA + a CME partner before committing._

## Why this is worth doing
Exam prep is a **one-time** sale to **students**. CME is a **recurring** sale to **every CAA, forever**.
- Every CAA must earn **50 CME hours every 2 years** to stay certified (NCCAA), and pay a **$295 CME
  registration fee** each cycle. That's a permanent, non-optional demand from the entire CAA workforce.
- It directly serves the **CAA / recert** half of MACPrep's audience — the half exam prep mostly misses.
- It fits MACPrep's DNA: our differentiator is **evidence-based, journal-sourced content**, which is
  exactly what good CME is. We'd be extending the brand, not inventing a new one.
- Our main competitor (Vigilant IQ) already monetizes CME — leaving it to them cedes recurring revenue
  and the recert relationship.

## The rules (NCCAA recert CME)
- **50 hours every 2 years.** Cycle is odd- or even-year based on initial certification year.
- **40 hours must be CAA Category I — Anesthesia CME** (the valuable, anesthesia-specific bucket).
- **10 hours** may be CAA Category II (general medicine / professional development).
- **$295** CME registration fee, due **June 1** of the registration year.
- NCCAA **randomly audits** submissions annually — accreditation/credit documentation must be airtight.
- (Separately, a **CDQ exam** is required first at 4 years, then every 10 — a future tie-in to our
  exam-prep product.)

## How Vigilant IQ does it (the model to match/beat)
- **6 case-based, question-driven modules** (airway failure, hemodynamic collapse, hemorrhage, drug
  reactions, OB emergencies, peds emergencies), 5 questions each.
- **10 AMA PRA Category 1 Credits™**, accredited via **joint providership with the University of
  Cincinnati** (an ACCME-accredited provider).
- **$199** individual; group pricing available.

## The path for MACPrep
MACPrep is not an accredited CME provider and doesn't need to become one. The standard route (and
exactly what Vigilant did):

1. **Joint providership with an ACCME-accredited provider** — a university anesthesiology department /
   medical school, a medical society, or a commercial CME company. They hold accreditation, handle
   compliance/documentation, and issue **AMA PRA Category 1 Credit**; MACPrep develops the clinical
   content. Confirm the partner's credits qualify as **NCCAA Category I — Anesthesia**.
2. **Build anesthesia Category I content** — case-based modules in our house style: evidence-based,
   **journal-sourced**, with a rationale per answer choice. This is our quality wedge applied to CME.
3. **MVP:** one strong 5–10 credit anesthesia module to validate demand and the partner workflow before
   building a full 40-credit catalog.
4. **Pricing:** Vigilant anchors at ~$199 / 10 credits. Options: match on price and win on
   transparency/sourcing, or bundle CME + exam prep into one MACPrep subscription that follows a CAA
   from school through every recert cycle (a stickier, higher-LTV play than either product alone).

## Work + risks to weigh
- **The accreditation partnership is the real work** — finding a willing ACCME-accredited partner and
  agreeing on revenue split, content ownership, and compliance responsibilities. Start here; everything
  else depends on it.
- **Audit exposure** — credit documentation must be airtight; the partner owns most of this, but our
  content must support it.
- **Content depth** — CME demands current, citable clinical content (our strength) but more of it.
