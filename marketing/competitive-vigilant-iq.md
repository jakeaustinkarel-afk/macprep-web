# Competitive Brief — Vigilant IQ (MACPrep's primary competitor)

_Last researched: 2026-06-21. Verify pricing/features against vigilantiq.com before quoting publicly._

## Who they are
- **Vigilant IQ** — NCCAA exam prep + CME for CAAs. https://vigilantiq.com
- **Founder: David Dunipace, CAA, MSA.** A practicing CAA, like Jake. (Associated entity: "Vigilant
  Anesthesia, LLC," Ball Ground, GA.)
- **Case Western connection (per Jake): UNCONFIRMED.** Dunipace is *not* listed in CWRU's current MSA
  leadership (Exec PD Shane Angus, CAA; DC PD Daniel Pistone, CAA). He may be former/affiliated faculty.
  Confirm before treating any CWRU campus as a competitor-controlled account.

## What they offer (their claims)
- **1,000+ questions**, "true NCCAA-style," blueprint-aligned to the NCCAA content outline.
- Written by "expert test writers / educators with psychometric design backgrounds."
- Explanations: "clear, evidence-based... backed by credible sources" — **but no per-question source
  links / citations published** on the product page.
- **Spaced repetition** ("Smart Review"), performance analytics by domain, Practice + Quiz modes,
  full-length simulated mock exams, mobile-friendly.
- **CME product line** for CAA recertification.
- **Group/program pricing** for "CAA programs, clinical sites, and group subscriptions" — i.e. they
  are *already* pitching the program-director channel.
- Pricing: $99 / 30d · $199 / 90d · $249 / 180d · $299 / yr (one-time). Was free until May 1, 2026
  (a land-grab to seed a user base before monetizing).

## Honest read: where we have parity
Vigilant matches more of our feature list than we'd like to assume. They *also* have spaced
repetition, blueprint alignment, domain analytics, and mock exams, and they're *already* selling to
programs. So "we have features X/Y/Z" is **not** a winning message — they have them too.

## Where MACPrep genuinely wins (defensible wedges)
1. **Verifiable journal sourcing on every question + transparency badge.** They say "evidence-based,
   trust us"; we *show the source link*. This is our sharpest, hardest-to-copy edge — lead with it.
2. **A rationale for *every answer choice*, including the wrong ones.** Their explanations appear to be
   one-per-question. Per-choice rationale is a deeper teaching tool. _(Verify against a live Vigilant
   question before claiming in writing.)_
3. **Metacognitive features** — confidence calibration + peer-percentage ("how others answered").
4. **Answer-slot balancing (A=B=C=D=E)** to remove position bias — a quality/rigor signal they don't
   advertise.
5. **A practicing CAA reviews every published item** as the SME (Jake).

## Strategic implications
- **"Built by a CAA" is no longer a differentiator** — their founder is a CAA too. Reposition the
  wedge to **rigor + transparency + teaching depth** (verifiable sourcing, per-choice rationale). This
  correction applies everywhere: site, social, and the program one-pager.
- **The program channel is contested, not greenfield.** Some directors may already know Dunipace or use
  Vigilant. Our program pitch must differentiate on *why our questions are more trustworthy/teachable*,
  not on existing. Never bash Dunipace — it's a tiny field where everyone knows everyone. Win on substance.
- **CME is a gap.** Vigilant monetizes CAA recert via CME; MACPrep doesn't offer CME. Worth evaluating
  as a future product line for the CAA (recert) half of our audience.
- **Pricing:** our paid tiers should be legible against their $99–$299 anchors. Decide deliberately
  whether to undercut, match, or price up on the transparency/quality story.
